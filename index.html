Cronology

The first car associated with Mercedes-Benz is the Benz Patent-Motorwagen, which was built in 1885 by Karl Benz.
This three-wheeled vehicle is widely regarded as the first practical modern automobile and was the first car put into production.
It was patented in January 1886 and unveiled in public later that years.

The Mercedes 35 HP, also known as Mercedes 35 PS, was a groundbreaking early car model designed in 1901 by Wilhelm Maybach and Paul Daimler for Emil Jellinek.

The 1954 Mercedes-Benz 300 SL “Gullwing” is a highly regarded sports car known for its distinctive design and performance.

The 1963 Mercedes-Benz 220 is part of the W111 series, which was produced between 1959 and 1971. This series included four-door sedans, two-door coupes, and cabriolets. For 1963, the 220 Series was available as a sedan, coupe, or convertible.

The 1975 Mercedes-Benz S-Class (W116): This model introduced several safety features and luxury amenities, setting a new standard for the segment..

The 1990 Mercedes-Benz W124: Known for its reliability and comfort, this model was a significant step in Mercedes-Benz’s modernization.

The 2009 Mercedes-Benz S400 BlueHYBRID: As the first production automotive hybrid to use a lithium-ion battery, this car marked a significant shift towards sustainable technology.

The 2009 Mercedes-Benz G-Class, also known as the G Wagon, is a full-size luxury SUV that stands out for its unique styling and off-road capabilities

The Mercedes-Benz G-Wagon, also known as the G-Class or G-Wagen, is a luxury SUV renowned for its distinctive boxy styling and robust off-road capabilities
